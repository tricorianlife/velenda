About these policies

Velenda is operated by Tricorian Pty Ltd and its related entities (together, Velenda, we, us, our), Australian companies delivering pharma raw materials intelligence, sourcing services and the Velenda workflow operating system.

The policies on this page govern your use of www.velenda.com, the Velenda platform, and any service you receive from us. They describe our commitment to quality, the personal information we handle, the cookies we use, and the legal terms under which this site is published.

We are bound by the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles. These policies are effective from April 2026 and apply to all interactions with Velenda from that date forward. We may update them from time to time; the version published here is the current version.

In short. If you visit our website, contact our team, or use Velenda as a customer or pilot partner, these policies tell you how we treat your information and what your rights are.
Section 02

Quality policy

Velenda is committed to delivering trustworthy, accurate and timely intelligence to the pharmaceutical manufacturers, formulators and supply chain teams who rely on us. Quality, in our context, means the data we hold is current and provable, the decisions our customers make on top of it are supported by evidence, and the services we operate around it are dependable.

Our commitments

  • Maintain the integrity, currency and traceability of the proprietary raw materials database that underpins Velenda and our procurement service.
  • Operate Velenda and Clara AI in line with applicable regulatory, ethical and contractual obligations across the regions our customers manufacture in.
  • Continuously improve our processes, tooling and controls through internal review, customer feedback and pilot-partner engagement.
  • Hold our suppliers and partners to the standards our customers expect of us.
  • Train and support our team so that quality is owned end-to-end, not delegated to a checklist.

This quality policy is reviewed regularly and refreshed whenever the scope of our services, the regulatory environment, or the technology underneath Velenda materially changes.

Section 03

Privacy statement

Velenda respects your privacy and is committed to handling personal information lawfully, fairly and transparently in accordance with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles.

Information we collect

We collect personal information only where it is reasonably necessary for our functions or activities, including when:

  • You visit www.velenda.com (technical information such as IP address, browser type, device characteristics, pages viewed, referring URL and timestamps);
  • You contact us through email, web forms, phone or in person (your name, organisation, role, contact details and the content of your enquiry);
  • You engage with us as a customer, pilot partner, supplier or service provider (commercial contact details, billing information, contractual records and operational data needed to deliver the engagement);
  • You apply to work with us (information you provide in your application, references and any pre-engagement checks consented to).

How we use your information

We use personal information to:

  • Respond to enquiries and deliver the services you have asked us to deliver;
  • Operate, maintain, secure and improve our website and the Velenda platform;
  • Manage commercial relationships, including billing, contracting and reporting;
  • Meet our legal, regulatory, audit and risk-management obligations;
  • Communicate updates that are directly relevant to a service you receive from us. Marketing communications are sent only where you have opted in, and you can opt out at any time.

How we share your information

We do not sell personal information. We may disclose information to trusted service providers (for example, hosting, analytics, payment processing or professional advisors) under written agreements that require them to protect it and use it only for the agreed purpose. We may also disclose information where we are required or authorised to do so by law.

Some of our service providers operate outside Australia. Where information is transferred overseas we take reasonable steps to ensure it is handled in a manner consistent with the Australian Privacy Principles.

How we store and protect your information

We hold personal information in secured systems with access limited to personnel who need it to perform their role. We use encryption in transit, hardened cloud infrastructure, role-based access control, audit logging and ongoing security review. Where information is no longer required for the purpose it was collected for, and there is no legal obligation to retain it, we securely destroy or de-identify it.

Your rights

You have the right to ask what personal information we hold about you, to request that we correct it, and to ask us to delete it where we are not required to keep it. To exercise these rights, contact us using the details below. We will respond within a reasonable time and may need to verify your identity before releasing information.

Complaints

If you believe we have mishandled your personal information, please contact us first at sales@tricorian.com so we can investigate and respond. If you are not satisfied with our response, you can refer the matter to the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) at www.oaic.gov.au.

Contact for privacy matters. Email sales@tricorian.com with the subject line "Privacy" and we will route your request to the right person.
Section 04

Cookies policy

Cookies are small text files placed on your device when you visit a website. We use them to make this site work, to understand how it is used, and to improve it.

Types of cookies we use

  • Essential cookies — required for the site to function (for example, remembering navigation state). These cannot be turned off in our system.
  • Analytical cookies — help us understand which pages are visited and how visitors move through the site, so we can improve content and structure.
  • Functionality cookies — remember preferences (such as previously dismissed messages) so you have a more consistent experience between visits.
  • Targeting cookies — set by us or third parties to understand the relevance of content. We use these sparingly, only where they support a legitimate purpose.

Third-party cookies

We may use reputable third-party analytics or advertising platforms which set their own cookies (for example, to measure campaign performance or aggregate traffic statistics). These providers act as independent data handlers and are bound by their own privacy policies, in addition to any contractual obligations they owe us.

Managing cookies

You can control cookies through your browser settings — accepting, refusing or deleting them at any time. Disabling some categories may affect how parts of this site behave. Most browsers also offer a "do not track" option which, where supported, we honour.
